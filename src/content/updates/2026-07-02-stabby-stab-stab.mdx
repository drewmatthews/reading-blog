So this thing wrapped up pretty nicely with a bow was unexpected for a book series that is so lauded. And I was expecting some kind of big cliff hanger. Apparently this was by design. Sure there are lots of plot points left unfinished, yet nothing that cant wait for future books. This one is nicely presented with a bow. You could read this and feel content to not read another. Online folks say this is the worst of the series. As a first introduction to books again. It's fine. Its not without its tropes. For me, it leans hard on them. And the authors language gets repetitive fast. The reoccurring descriptions I find boring the most. The teeth(oh the teeth). I'm sure there are more but once I hear them. I can't imagine re-reading them is any easier. There are sections that I wish didn't exist. Admittedly, I'm not a writer. But I would have edited out some. Pacing seems to be my hardest problem and then there are nearly no explanations for some things and then great explanations to others. There is no middle ground. Speed through some details or story points and drag others out. Great history and battles happened and seemingly glossed over. A great battle seemingly strewn aside.
